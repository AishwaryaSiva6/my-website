01 · The Problem

International students face a broken system

"There is no fast, intuitive, and compatibility-driven way to find roommates or housing."

Current platforms like Facebook groups, Craigslist, and WhatsApp were designed for listing, not for matching. They have no compatibility filters, no verification, and no mutual consent mechanism before someone can message you.

Unorganized and cluttered

Facebook groups mix housing posts with event announcements, community updates, and spam. Finding a real listing requires scrolling through hundreds of irrelevant posts.

Incomplete or outdated listings

Craigslist and broker sites are full of listings filled months ago. No platform has a real-time availability signal.

No trust or compatibility filters

None of the existing platforms match on lifestyle. You can filter by price and location but not on sleep schedule, cleanliness habits, or cultural preferences.

02 · The Product

Tinder for roommates, but smarter

Built on a familiar swipe mechanic but designed around real-world compatibility under financial and logistical pressure.

Feature 1

Mobile-first matching interface

Intuitive swipe-based discovery. Right for interested, left to pass. The interaction pattern is zero-learning-curve.

Feature 2 · Key Differentiator

Mutual match before messaging

No unsolicited contact. Both parties must express interest before any conversation starts. Eliminates spam, harassment, and anxiety.

Feature 3 · Competitive Moat

Lifestyle compatibility scoring

Structured matching on sleep schedule, cleanliness habits, social life, study hours, and cultural preferences. A compatibility score is visible on every profile.

Feature 4

Dual marketplace

Two unified sections: Roommates (person-to-person matching) and Housing (rooms, units, landlords). One app handles both sides.

Feature 5

Verified-only profiles

Student and work email verification plus image upload. Fake listings and catfishing are eliminated at onboarding.

04 · The Ask

$50,000 pre-seed raise

Four milestones across 12 months to prove product-market fit before a seed raise.

Months 1 to 3

Ship the MVP

Launch functional app. Core features: compatibility quiz, email verification, mutual matching. Target: 200 verified profiles at launch.

Months 3 to 6

Boston university pilot

Partner with 2+ Boston universities. Activate ambassador program. Target: 500 verified users and first paid conversions.

Months 6 to 9

Validate unit economics

Reach 1,000 active users. Confirm real-world LTV:CAC of 2.83x and ARPU of $11 across all three premium features.

Months 9 to 12

Growth signal for seed

Hit 10,000 users and $80K Year 1 revenue. Lock in 3+ university partnerships. Begin expansion roadmap.
